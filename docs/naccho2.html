NACCHO Version 2.0

Published

June 24, 2024

The National Association of County & City Health Officials (NACCHO) 2.0 project officially started in October 2023. The NACCHO 2.0 data that is presented in this section covers all EIP encounters at UCMC ED from October 1st, 2023 through June 30th, 2024. During this time, there were a total of 4108 encounters within EIP, with 3395 unique patients. The majority of this report will focus on the number of unique patients for several reasons, but will also include total encounter details to highlight the total number of screenings that occur within the ED during this time.

The table below shows the metrics stated for each of the objectives for the NACCHO 2.0 grant. As stated earlier, there were a total of 4108 encounters at UCMC ED during the project period, with a total of 3395 unique patients. Substance use disorder (SUD) screening is defined as anyone who at least answered a question on the EIP assessment about their substance use history or experience. This includes the Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test (AUDIT-C) for alcohol and the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) Modified Alcohol, Smoking and Substance Involvement Screening Test (ASSIST) measure for other substances. Additional questions used when determining substance use screening include tobacco use history and fentanyl test strip (FTS) and other harm reduction tool awareness. There were a total of 1263 patients that were screened for SUD (37.2% of all patients, 1263/3395). An individual is determined to be positive for a SUD if they meet criteria on either the AUDIT-C or the NM-ASSIST. There were a total of 453 patients that screened positive for SUD for at least one substance (35.9% of all patients who were screened for SUD, 453/1263). The NACCHO 2.0 project is specifically looking at those individuals who screened positive for opioid use disorder (OUD), and there were a total of 111 patients that screened positive specifically for an OUD (3.3% of all patients, 111/3395; or 24.5% of all those that screened positive for SUD, 111/453).

Objective 1

The first objective on the 2023 NACCHO 2.0 workplan has both operational components and data that can be evaluated. Objective 1 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Perform follow-up calls to patients with OUD that have been seen in the ED to determine rates of follow-up to treatment facilities. Specifically, objective 1c encompasses the data component and states that we will “Conduct follow-up calls for all people with OUD seen in the ED.” Therefore, we can look at the total number of individuals that screen positive at UCMC ED using the NIDA-Modified ASSIST tool used in the EIP assessment, and how many of those individuals are followed-up with after their encounter to determine engagement in treatment. Also, for Objective 1 under the sustainability component, it discusses the importance of peer involvement, so we can also analyze the number of these OUD screening encounters where a peer is involved.

For screening for SUD/OUD within EIP, the NM-ASSIST is used and is located within the overall EIP assessment. For this project, we also added the NM-ASSIST to the Linkage to Care page so that NACCHO staff involved with follow-ups can complete the screening tools for those who endorse opioid use but did not have a complete assessment to determine whether they meet criteria for OUD. Therefore, when providing numbers for screening and positivity, this includes both ASSIST tools. As mentioned above, the total number of patients that received a SUD assessment was 1263 patients (37.2% of all patients, 1263/3395), with a total of 453 that screened positive for any type of SUD (35.9% of all those assessed for SUD, 453/1263). The total number of patients that screened positive specifically for OUD (street/prescription opioids) was 111 patients (24.5% of all those SUD positive, 111/453), which is 3.3% of all patients seen during this project (111/3395) or 24.5% of all patients who screened positive for SUD (111/453).

Therefore, there are a total of 111 patients that are eligible for follow-up for this NACCHO 2.0 grant. The total number of patients where contact was attempted was 55 (49.5% of all those OUD positive, 55/111). The initial contact is 14 days after their ED encounter. The total number of contact attempts made at day 14 was 32 (58.2% of all contact attempts, 32/55). If contact at day 14 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 15, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 15 was 1 (1.8% of all contact attempts, 1/55). If contact at day 15 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 16, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 16 was 3 (5.5% of all contact attempts, 3/55). If contact at day 16 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was suspended until day 30, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 30 was 17 (30.9% of all contact attempts, 17/55). If contact at day 30 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 31, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 31 was 0 (0.0% of all contact attempts, 0/55). If contact at day 31 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 32, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 32 was 2 (3.6% of all contact attempts, 2/55).

Out of the total number of patients that screened positive for OUD (111 patients), there were a total of 27 patients that were successfully contacted (49.1% of all those where contact was attempted, 27/55). The total number of successful contact attempts made at day 14 was 18 (66.7% of all successful contact attempts, 18/27). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 15 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/27). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 16 was 1 (3.7% of all successful contact attempts, 1/27). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 30 was 7 (25.9% of all successful contact attempts, 7/27). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 31 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/27). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 32 was 1 (3.7% of all successful contact attempts, 1/27).

Finally, NACCHO staff are meant to confirm whether the individual is established with treatment for OUD. For all of those individuals where our NACCHO staff was able to successfully make contact, they have been able to confirm that 7 patients are established with treatment for OUD (25.9% of all successful contact attempts, 7/27), which is 6.3% of all patients that screened positive for an OUD.

As for peer encounters, there were a total of 336 patients that had an encounter with one of the EIP Peer Support Staff at UCMC ED during this time (9.9% of all patients, 336/3395). There was a total of 32 patients that screened positive for OUD that had an encounter with a peer during their ED visit (0.9% of all patients, 32/3395; or 28.8% of all those positive for OUD, 32/111).



Objective 2

Objective 2 for the NACCHO 2.0 workplan further analyzes the ED encounter by looking at the provision of buprenorphine or naloxone through the ED. Objective 2 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Analyze data on provision of buprenorphine and/or naloxone. The data regarding buprenorphine and/or naloxone distribution during this encounter comes from ED-based data, not internal EIP data. The way these OUD screening encounters are matched with the ED data to know whether buprenorphine/naloxone was provided is through the CSN for the ED encounter. This data analysis is still a work in progress.


Objective 3

Objective 3 for the NACCHO 2.0 workplan covers data collected through focus groups. Objective 3 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Conduct focus groups with ED patients to determine drivers of satisfaction with care. At this time, there is no data to present or analyze for this objective.


Objective 4

Objective 4 for the NACCHO 2.0 workplan covers data that is collected using satisfaction surveys for people with OUD. Objective 4 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Design and conduct satisfaction surveys, targeted at people with OUD. At this time, there is no data to present or analyze for this objective.
