NACCHO V2.0

The National Association of County & City Health Officials (NACCHO) 2.0 project officially started in October 2023. The NACCHO 2.0 data that is presented in this section covers all EIP encounters at UCMC ED from October 1st, 2023 through June 30th, 2024. During this time, there were a total of 2433 encounters within EIP, with 2067 unique patients. The majority of this report will focus on the number of unique patients for several reasons, but will also include total encounter details to highlight the total number of screenings that occur within the ED during this time.

The first objective on the 2023 NACCHO 2.0 workplan has both operational components and data that can be evaluated. Objective 1 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Perform follow-up calls to patients with OUD that have been seen in the ED to determine rates of follow-up to treatment facilities. Specifically, objective 1c encompasses the data component and states that we will “Conduct follow-up calls for all people with OUD seen in the ED.” Therefore, we can look at the total number of individuals that screen positive at UCMC ED using the NIDA-Modified ASSIST tool used in the EIP assessment, and how many of those individuals are followed-up with after their encounter to determine engagement in treatment. Also, for Objective 1 under the sustainability component, it discusses the importance of peer involvement, so we can also analyze the number of these OUD screening encounters where a peer is involved.

For screening for SUD/OUD within EIP, the NIDA-Modified ASSIST is used and is located within the overall EIP assessment. For this project, we also added the NM-ASSIST to the Linkage to Care page so that NACCHO staff involved with follow-ups can complete the screening tools for those who endorse opioid use but did not have a complete assessment to determine whether they meet criteria for OUD. Therefore, when providing numbers for screening and positivity, this includes both ASSIST tools. The total number of patients that received a SUD assessment was 770 patients (37.3% of all patients, 770/2067), with a total of 280 that screened positive for any type of SUD (36.4% of all those assessed for SUD, 280/770). The total number of patients that screened positive specifically for OUD (street/prescription opioids) was 74 (26.4% of all those SUD positive, 74/280), which is 3.6% of all patients seen during this project (74/2067).

Therefore, there are a total of 74 patients that are eligible for follow-up for this NACCHO 2.0 grant. The total number of patients where contact was attempted was 44 (59.5% of all those OUD positive, 44/74). The initial contact is 14 days after their ED encounter. The total number of contact attempts made at day 14 was 26 (59.1% of all contact attempts, 26/44). If contact at day 14 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 15, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 15 was 0 (0.0% of all contact attempts, 0/44). If contact at day 15 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 16, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 16 was 0 (0.0% of all contact attempts, 0/44). If contact at day 16 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was suspended until day 30, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 30 was 0 (0.0% of all contact attempts, 0/44). If contact at day 30 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 31, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 31 was 0 (0.0% of all contact attempts, 0/44). If contact at day 31 was unsuccessful, or not initiated, then contact was made at day 32, and the total number of patients who were attempted to contact at day 32 was 0 (0.0% of all contact attempts, 0/44).

Out of the total number of patients that screened positive for OUD (74 patients), there were a total of 20 patients that were successfully contacted (45.5% of all those where contact was attempted, 20/44). The total number of successful contact attempts made at day 14 was 14 (70.0% of all successful contact attempts, 14/20). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 15 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/20). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 16 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/20). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 30 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/20). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 31 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/20). The total number of patients who were successfully contacted at day 32 was 0 (0.0% of all successful contact attempts, 0/20).

Finally, NACCHO staff are meant to confirm whether the individual is established with treatment for OUD. For all of those individuals where our NACCHO staff was able to successfully make contact, they have been able to confirm that 6 patients are established with treatment for OUD (30.0% of all successful contact attempts, 6/20), which is 8.1% of all patients that screened positive for an OUD.




Objective 2 for the NACCHO 2.0 workplan further analyzes the ED encounter by looking at the provision of buprenorphine or naloxone through the ED. Objective 2 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Analyze data on provision of buprenorphine and/or naloxone. The data regarding buprenorphine and/or naloxone distribution during this encounter comes from ED-based data, not internal EIP data. The way these OUD screening encounters are matched with the ED data to know whether buprenorphine/naloxone was provided is through the CSN for the ED encounter. This data analysis is still a work in progress.


Objective 3 for the NACCHO 2.0 workplan covers data collected through focus groups. Objective 3 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Conduct focus groups with ED patients to determine drivers of satisfaction with care. At this time, there is no data to present or analyze for this objective.


Objective 4 for the NACCHO 2.0 workplan covers data that is collected using satisfaction surveys for people with OUD. Objective 4 for the NACCHO 2.0 grant was Design and conduct satisfaction surveys, targeted at people with OUD. At this time, there is no data to present or analyze for this objective.
